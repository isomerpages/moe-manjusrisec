<p>&nbsp;Welcome to the website of Manjusri Secondary School (MJR).&nbsp;
We are at present the only government-aided mission school benefiting from
the support of the Singapore Buddhist Federation in adopting a holistic
approach towards educating students from varied backgrounds.&nbsp; Pupils
from our affiliated primary schools (Maha Bodhi School &amp; Mee Toh School)
made up about one-third of the Sec 1 (2026) cohort.</p>
<p><u>Mission and Vision</u>
</p>
<p>Our school sees its mission as nurturing&nbsp;Passionate Learners&nbsp;and
Compassionate Leaders who are future-ready and anchored in values.&nbsp;
We aim to develop young people to be confident engaged learners who can
face future challenges with composure and resilience; as well as responsible
citizens who possess a moral compass and a sense of their purpose and place
in society.&nbsp;
<br>
</p>
<p>Our vision is to be a Vibrant Learning Community distinguished by Wisdom,
Conduct, Benevolence and Aspiration.&nbsp; Teachers strive to be respected
for their professionalism, expertise and integrity (Wisdom &amp; Conduct)
in building a culture of learning.&nbsp; As an educational institution,
we seek to embody a spirit of charity and kindness balanced against the
pursuit of excellence (Benevolence &amp; Aspiration).&nbsp;&nbsp;
<br>
<br><u>Hybrid Class Grouping</u>
</p>
<p>You will be interested to know that we are piloting an innovative hybrid
way of grouping form classes and teaching groups at the lower secondary
level.&nbsp; Beginning with the Sec 1 classes (2026), the school is grouping
students in <strong>same-sex form classes</strong> but in <strong>co-educational teaching groups</strong>.&nbsp;&nbsp;
In this arrangement, the students will attend <strong>common</strong>  <strong>curriculum</strong> lessons,
(e.g. Character &amp; Citizenship Education, Physical Education, Art, Music,
etc), in same-sex form classes.&nbsp; This is to allow us to create new
opportunities in their social-emotional learning and personal development.&nbsp;
Students will continue to attend G1/G2/G3 <strong>core</strong>  <strong>curriculum</strong> lessons,
(e.g. Mathematics, Science, Languages, Humanities) in mixed co-educational
teaching groups.</p>
<p><u>Distinctive Programmes</u>
</p>
<p>The school’s key programmes include (1)&nbsp;<strong>Smart City &amp; Sustainable Technologies</strong>&nbsp;-
introducing students through guided team projects in the use of coding,
design thinking and data analytics to create solutions for improving our
community or sustaining an eco-friendly urban environment; &nbsp;(2)&nbsp;<strong>Learning Life-skills through the Arts</strong>&nbsp;-
strengthening students’ exposure to and appreciation of various art forms
(music, art and drama) as an avenue for self-expression, team work and
developing their confidence and imagination; (3) <strong>Bilingual Literacy</strong> –
promoting bilingualism and strengthening the communication literacy of
students in English and Mother Tongue Languages.&nbsp; The school offers
both Higher Chinese and Chinese Literature for students with the aptitude
and interest.
<br>
</p>
<p>MJR has an active and vibrant <strong>co-curricular curriculum</strong> in
support of student character development.&nbsp; Prestigious Mee Toh Foundation
Arts Scholarships are awarded annually to students who can balance their
studies with active contribution in the performing or visual arts.&nbsp;
Since 2024, Kwan Im Thong Hood Cho Temple Scholarships for excellence in
Uniformed Groups leadership as well as in Sports have been established.&nbsp;&nbsp;
The school also welcomes Direct School Admission (Sec) applicants in the
areas of Chinese Dance, Chinese Orchestra, Concert Band, Choir, Calligraphy
and Wushu.</p>
<p><u>Positive Student Experiences</u>
</p>
<p>We were heartened to receive strong affirmation from our students about
their schooling in the biennial <strong>Quality of School Experience</strong> survey
findings.&nbsp; Their responses covered domains such as (i) developing
students holistically, (ii) engaging for learning, (iii) recognizing students,
(iv) disciplinary practices, (v) fostering teacher-student relationships
and (vi) fostering peer relationships.&nbsp; Students’ feedback on our
school’s structures and processes exceeded the national cohort comparative
indices in 2025 and are a continuation of upward trends over a five-year
period.</p>
<p>My teachers and I look forward to collaborating with parents, partners
and community to provide a balanced all-round education for our pupils;
and to ensure that they are ready for the future as morally upright individuals
and responsible contributors in society.</p>
<p>Sim Chong Boon</p>
<p>Principal</p>
